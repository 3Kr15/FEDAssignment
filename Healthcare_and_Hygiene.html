Hygiene


Rabbits do not necessarily need to be bathe, however, the cleanliness of the environment they live is extremely important. Regular grooming, examining of ears and dental problems and parasite prevention are regular things to do to keep a rabbit safe in a dust-free environment.

Things to Know!

As rabbits are considered fragile compared to other pets making this a perception rooted in certain characteristics of their physiology and behavior. In addition, rabbits are prey animals which explained why they can be easily frightened or stressed.

Grooming practices such as brushing and nail trimming are a must to prevent matting and discomfort. Regular exercise and mental stimulation contribute to a rabbit’s physical and mental health. That also include to avoid locking a rabbit in a cage which rabbits lose their nature. Rabbits are social animals so it is important for them to have company, be it a human friend, another rabbit or pet.

Regular veterinary check-ups are essential to monitor overall health and preventive measures, including vaccinations and spaying/neutering, play a vital role in ensuring a rabbit’s longevity. Handling waste responsibly and incorporating flea and tick control further contribute to a clean and hygienic living environment for pet rabbits.

The above information are just a summary of the important things to know to become a bun-mom or dad! It is always important to notice signs from a bunny and reach the vet when our furry babies are showing signs!
